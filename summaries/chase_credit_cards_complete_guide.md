# Chase Credit Cards - Complete Guide

## 1. Chase 5/24 Rule

### Definition
If you've opened 5 or more personal credit card accounts (from any bank) in the past 24 months, you will be automatically denied for most Chase cards.

### Key Details
- Counts personal cards reporting to credit bureaus
- Business cards typically **DON'T** count towards 5/24
- Authorized user accounts may count (can sometimes appeal)
- Closed cards still count if opened within 24 months
- Affects both personal AND business Chase card applications

---

## 2. Chase Approval Algorithm & Strategies

### Recent Changes
- Chase has tightened approval criteria, especially for business cards
- More scrutiny on applications

### Approval Factors
- Number of existing Chase cards
- Spending patterns on existing cards
- Expected business spend
- Total credit line exposure (typically max 50% of reported income)

### Approval Tips
✓ Have a Chase banking relationship
✓ Large balances in Chase checking/savings may help
✓ Space out applications
✓ Be prepared for more difficult reconsideration
✓ Show consistent spending on existing Chase cards

---

## 3. Chase Credit Limit Rules

### Guideline
Chase typically won't approve total credit lines exceeding 50% of reported income across all your Chase cards.

### Management Strategies
- Lower credit limits on existing cards **BEFORE** applying for new ones
- Can move credit between existing cards during application
- Closing business cards doesn't significantly impact credit score after one year

---

## 4. Reconsideration Process

- Becoming more challenging
- No longer guaranteed approval even if under 5/24 with good credit
- May require detailed explanation of business activities
- Harder to negotiate or overturn initial denials

## 6. Chase Ultimate Rewards Program

### How It Works
- Earn points through Freedom, Sapphire, and Ink cards
- Pool points between personal and business cards
- Transfer 1:1 to airline and hotel partners

### Transfer Partners

**Airlines:**
- United
- Southwest
- British Airways
- Air France/KLM
- Virgin Atlantic
- Aeroplan
- Singapore Airlines
- Iberia

**Hotels:**
- World of Hyatt (considered most valuable)
- IHG
- Marriott

### Redemption Strategies
✓ Transfer to partners for best value (often 1.5-2+ cents per point)
✓ World of Hyatt transfers often provide 2+ cents per point value
✓ **AVOID:** Portal redemptions (usually only 1.25-1.5 cents per point)
✗ Pay Yourself Back: 1.5 cents per point (decent but not optimal)

## 8. Best Practices & Strategy

## 10. Product Change Options

### Downgrade Paths
- Sapphire Reserve → Sapphire Preferred → Freedom Flex/Unlimited
- Sapphire Preferred → Freedom Flex/Unlimited
- Ink Preferred → Ink Unlimited or Ink Cash

### Benefits
- Avoid annual fees
- Keep account history
- Maintain Ultimate Rewards points
- No hard inquiry

### Timing
- Wait until after first year to avoid clawback
- Can request after annual fee posts for refund
