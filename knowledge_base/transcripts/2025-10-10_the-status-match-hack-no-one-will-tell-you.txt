This video is purely for educational purposes and I'm not necessarily ever advocating to abuse or go around the terms and service agreement of any airlines or hotels. A lot of airlines and hotels have status match or challenge programs where if you have status at a competing airline or hotel chain, they will match or give you temporary status while you complete some sort of challenge. This of course requires you to hold the equivalent status. So for example, let's say United is doing a status challenge. They may say, well, if you hold American Executive Platinum, we will temporarily match you to United 1K status. But the thing is they don't actually have any way of verifying this outside of screenshots that you provide to the airline. So if someone provides a screenshot saying they are executive platinum with x amount of miles flown or whatever, United usually will take that at face value. There are exceptions and there are airlines that do sometimes try to verify outside of the traditional norm of just looking at the screenshots. Maybe they will actually contact the loyalty department of a competing airline. Sometimes there is collaboration agreements, but the vast majority of the time a screenshot of status is as good as having the status.
